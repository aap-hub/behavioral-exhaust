# Dialectic Round 1: Critic Response

The advocate response improves the thesis in one important way: it stops trying to resurrect the claims that were correctly retracted, and it puts more weight on matched-task and within-task evidence. That is the right direction. Still, most of the new evidence is best read as suggestive strengthening, not as a clean rescue of the main thesis.

## Critic's Hierarchy:
1. Strongest new support: within-task replicate evidence (promising but preliminary)
2. Useful descriptive support: matched-task disagreement analysis and thinking/message dissociation
3. Exploratory only: threshold cutpoints, Fisher rescues, and gate distributions

## Key Critiques:
- Finding 1 (disagreement): n=5 tasks, pseudoreplication risk. Need task-level meta-analysis.
- Finding 2 (threshold): post-hoc threshold selection. Need pre-specified cutpoint.
- Finding 3 (dissociation): real but partly genre difference. Need length-matched comparison.
- Finding 4 (replicates): best evidence but fragile (1 task, 9 replicates). Need cross-task combination.
- Finding 5 (Fisher rescue): Fisher and permutation answer different questions. Permutation failure dominates.
- Finding 6 (gate): 53% blocking for 15pp lift is steep. Need cost model and out-of-sample eval.
